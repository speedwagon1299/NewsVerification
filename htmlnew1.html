About Us

Srihari built an ML model to extract key facts from user-submitted article URLs and integrated the entire backend system.

Hima crafted the website’s styling, ensuring a user-friendly experience. Together, we deliver a seamless fact-checking tool.



Working

In our News Verification project, there are two Large Language models used to effectively produce the desired output of facts and reasonable validation.
1. User begins by entering the URL of an article they want to verify.
2. The first model, a PEGASUS model finetuned using Low Rank Adaptation (LoRA), generates facts from the article, identifying key claims made.
3. After postprocessing, these facts are sent to the second stage. For each fact, upto 5 relevant searches are found.
Each fact is tested against the contents of each similar article through an uncased DistilBERT model finetuned on Multi-Genre Natural Language Inference (MNLI), which analyzes the content of these articles and checks whether the extracted facts are supported, contradicted, or inconclusive.
4. The results are then presented to the user, providing a clear indication of the factual accuracy of the original article.
